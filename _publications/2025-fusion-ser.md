<h3>üìù Abstract</h3>
<p class="abstract-text">
<strong>Background:</strong> Speech emotion recognition (SER) has significant potential for clinical applications, yet existing models often suffer from poor reproducibility and limited generalizability across different datasets, speakers, and recording conditions.<br><br>
<strong>Objectives:</strong> We propose an Intelligent Fusion Network (IFN) that integrates multiple feature extraction pathways to achieve robust, reproducible, and generalizable SER performance suitable for medical and clinical deployment.<br><br>
<strong>Methods:</strong> The proposed IFN combines acoustic features (MFCCs, spectral features), prosodic features, and deep representations from pre-trained models through an adaptive fusion mechanism. The network employs attention-based weighting to dynamically emphasize the most informative feature streams for each input sample. Cross-corpus validation protocols were implemented to rigorously evaluate generalizability.<br><br>
<strong>Results:</strong> The IFN achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple benchmark SER datasets, demonstrating significantly improved cross-corpus generalizability compared to single-stream baselines. Reproducibility analyses confirm consistent performance across experimental runs and dataset splits.<br><br>
<strong>Conclusions:</strong> The proposed fusion architecture provides a reproducible and generalizable solution for clinical SER applications. The adaptive fusion mechanism effectively leverages complementary information from multiple feature streams, enabling robust emotion recognition across diverse acoustic environments.
</p>
