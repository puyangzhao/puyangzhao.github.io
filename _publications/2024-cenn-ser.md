<h3>üìù Abstract</h3>
<p class="abstract-text">
<strong>Background:</strong> Standard neural network architectures for speech emotion recognition often lack the ability to capture part-whole relationships in emotional speech patterns. Additionally, conventional evaluation metrics may not adequately capture the nuanced performance differences between SER systems.<br><br>
<strong>Objectives:</strong> We introduce CENN, a Capsule-Enhanced Neural Network, along with innovative evaluation metrics designed to provide a more comprehensive and clinically meaningful assessment of SER performance.<br><br>
<strong>Methods:</strong> CENN integrates capsule layers into a deep neural network backbone, enabling the model to learn hierarchical part-whole relationships in speech features. Novel metrics are proposed to evaluate emotion boundary detection accuracy, emotional intensity estimation, and cross-speaker generalization, complementing traditional accuracy and F1 scores.<br><br>
<strong>Results:</strong> CENN outperforms comparable architectures on multiple benchmark datasets. The proposed metrics reveal performance dimensions not captured by standard measures, providing more actionable insights for clinical system development.<br><br>
<strong>Conclusions:</strong> Capsule enhancement combined with comprehensive evaluation metrics advances both the capability and interpretability of SER systems for knowledge-based clinical decision support.
</p>
