<h3>üìù Abstract</h3>
<p class="abstract-text">
<strong>Background:</strong> Accurate speech emotion recognition requires effective processing of pre-attentive auditory features that capture subtle emotional cues before higher-level cognitive processing.<br><br>
<strong>Objectives:</strong> This work proposes a pre-attentive speech signal processing framework incorporating adaptive routing mechanisms to enhance emotion recognition performance in clinical and human-computer interaction settings.<br><br>
<strong>Methods:</strong> We introduce a multi-pathway processing architecture that mimics pre-attentive auditory processing stages. An adaptive routing mechanism dynamically selects and weights processing pathways based on input speech characteristics. The framework integrates low-level acoustic features with learned representations through a hierarchical attention mechanism.<br><br>
<strong>Results:</strong> The proposed framework demonstrates competitive performance on standard SER benchmarks, with the adaptive routing mechanism showing particular advantages for diverse and noisy speech conditions encountered in real-world clinical applications.<br><br>
<strong>Conclusions:</strong> Pre-attentive processing with adaptive routing offers a biologically-inspired approach to SER that improves robustness and clinical applicability.
</p>
