<h3>üìù Abstract</h3>
<p class="abstract-text">
<strong>Background:</strong> Accurate solar power prediction is critical for integrating renewable energy into electrical grids. Australia's solar energy market provides a rich dataset for evaluating prediction methodologies given the country's high solar irradiance and growing photovoltaic capacity.<br><br>
<strong>Objectives:</strong> This study provides a systematic comparison of statistical and machine learning methods for solar power prediction using data from the Australian solar power market, aiming to identify the most effective approaches for different forecasting horizons.<br><br>
<strong>Methods:</strong> We evaluate a suite of methods including ARIMA and seasonal decomposition (statistical), alongside gradient boosting machines, random forests, and neural network approaches (machine learning). Features include historical power output, meteorological variables (irradiance, temperature, cloud cover), and temporal indicators. Models are assessed across short-term (hourly), medium-term (daily), and longer-term prediction horizons.<br><br>
<strong>Results:</strong> Machine learning approaches, particularly gradient boosting, outperform classical statistical methods for short-to-medium term prediction, while hybrid approaches combining statistical and ML components show promise for longer horizons. Feature importance analysis reveals that direct irradiance measurements are the strongest predictors.<br><br>
<strong>Conclusions:</strong> The comparative analysis provides practical guidance for selecting solar power prediction methods in the Australian market context, with implications for renewable energy grid management more broadly.
</p>
